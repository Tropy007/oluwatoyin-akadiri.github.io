Market intelligence case study

Competitor Pricing & Data Intelligence

A structured assessment of the medical-English and healthcare-language training market, designed to turn competitor data into clear positioning, pricing, and growth decisions.

Role: Data Analytics & Insights InternFocus: Spain + global marketMethods: Pricing, feature, threat, and gap analysis

Business question

Where can a differentiated healthcare-language platform win?

The project evaluated how established providers compete across business model, price, geography, delivery method, AI capability, certification, and target customer. The analysis combined a full-market scan with a focused comparison of CESM, Kleinson, and LL Medical English.

The goal was not simply to list competitors. It was to identify defensible market gaps and convert them into practical product, pricing, and go-to-market recommendations.

Market structure

Four models shape the competitive landscape.

01

Corporate / FUNDAE

Employer-funded and subsidized training, commonly priced by instructional hour.

02

App subscriptions

Recurring self-service products built around mobile access and individual learning.

03

Online courses

Structured programs sold as individual courses, bundles, or annual access.

04

Specialist services

Instructor-led or niche offerings with higher-touch delivery and premium positioning.

Key findings

Data revealed three high-value strategic gaps.

01

AI remains underdeveloped

Only two competitors demonstrated meaningful AI integration, leaving space for scenario-based clinical conversation tools and scalable personalized practice.

02

B2C and B2B remain disconnected

No competitor served individual subscribers and FUNDAE-supported corporate buyers equally well, creating room for a coordinated dual-market offer.

03

Mobile-first blended delivery is limited

The market showed a gap between convenient app-based learning and higher-value certification or instructor-supported experiences.

Pricing intelligence

A clear middle-market opportunity.

Individual subscriptions clustered around an annual sweet spot of approximately €120–€300, while corporate FUNDAE models commonly ranged from €7.50–€13 per instructional hour. This supported a value-led individual position near €12.99 per month, paired with a separate corporate route.

Positioning principleMatch accessible market pricing while differentiating through AI-supported clinical practice and dual B2C/B2B scalability.

Recommended strategy

From market evidence to an actionable roadmap.

  1. 1

    Launch a focused B2C offer

    Lead with accessible monthly pricing and AI-supported clinical conversations.

  2. 2

    Build corporate market access

    Pursue FUNDAE accreditation and pilot the offer with Spanish healthcare organizations.

  3. 3

    Strengthen professional credibility

    Layer certification, CME, or continuing-education partnerships onto the core product.

  4. 4

    Expand geographically

    Use the scalable platform model to test adjacent EU markets after validating Spain.

Outcome

A decision-ready market intelligence package.

The final deliverables included a 14-competitor dataset, a three-competitor deep dive, pricing and threat matrices, four executive visualizations, and a structured set of growth recommendations for product and commercial planning.
